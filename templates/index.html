Background

Menstrual disorders are a major issue for women around the world, with around 2.5 million women being diagnosed every year. These symptoms range from behavioral, emotional, and physical symptoms. Particularly, many menstruation issues involve dysmenorrhea, which is defined as excessive period pains.

While secondary dysmenorrhea causes major issues for those experiencing periods, many doctors and medical professionals downplay the pain and symptoms patients feel. Additionally, many women avoid discussing potential problems with their menstruation cycle in order to avoid further marginalization and adhere to menstrual etiquette.

Our Sensor

Our mission is to make the diagnosis of common untreated menstrual disorders, in particular endometriosis, more effective and accessible to the average consumer. EndoSens provides users with a way to comfortably and continuously collect data to aid health care professionals and users alike in early diagnosis of chronic menstrual conditions.

  • Comfortable graphic user interface that ensures accessibility and inclusivity
  • Sleek and modern design that allows the user to accurately collect data without sacrificing any quality of life
  • Repeatedly tested to ensure an accurate and informative experience

How to Use

Methodology

Endosens uses a Myoware 2.0 muscle sensor, which detects electrical activity in a muscle using conductive pads placed on the skin. When the muscle is activated, individual fibers within it receive electrical impulses, causing them to contract.

  • The stronger the contraction, the more fibers are active simultaneously, resulting in greater electrical activity
  • The sensor's signal processing hardware converts this noisy signal into a voltage level, which correlates with the effort exerted to activate the muscle

With this, we track abdominal muscle spasms and cramps, which in turn correspond to the abdominal pain that people with secondary dysmenorrhea feel.
